Netherlands
PM Jetten minority coalition (D66 / VVD / CDA) · Sworn in 23 Feb 2026
Security Domain
Civil unrest
Low
StructuralME Spillover
  • Protests are frequent but peaceful and managed; no sustained clashes or disorder in the past 1–2 weeks.
  • Rival Iran war demonstrations in The Hague (late March) required police separation of opposing groups — contained without incident.
  • Protest frequency has approximately doubled since October 2023, largely attributable to the Middle East conflict; volume is elevated but character remains non-violent.
  • National civil service strike scheduled for 14 April — expect disruption to public services, not public disorder.
Political violence & terrorism
Moderate
StructuralEmergingME Spillover
  • NCTV Level 4/5 "Substantial" reconfirmed Dec 2025; held continuously since Dec 2023.
  • 7 April Dutch UN submission flagged accelerating online radicalisation among minors and rise of nihilistic extremism with no specific ideological anchor.
  • HAYI-attributed attacks: Jewish school (Amsterdam, Mar), synagogue (Rotterdam, Mar), pro-Israel centre (Nijkerk, 1 Apr) — all low-yield devices, all at night, zero casualties.
  • Dutch prosecutors confirmed Rotterdam attack was "aimed at instilling fear in the Jewish community" — targeted intimidation, not mass-casualty intent.
  • No indiscriminate attacks on general civilian or business targets in the past 1–2 weeks.
  • At least 10 arrests in the Netherlands; suspects are predominantly teenagers recruited via Snapchat/Telegram.
Violent crime
Low
Structural
  • One of the lowest homicide rates in Europe (0.6 per 100,000).
  • CBS 2025 Safety Monitor confirms violent crime incidence is stable year-on-year.
  • Amsterdam and The Hague police issued routine advisories this week for distraction thefts in tourist areas — a recurring seasonal pattern, not an escalation.
Political Domain
Rule of law
Low
Structural
  • Independent judiciary functioning normally; no systemic concerns around arbitrary arrest or detention.
  • Civil society debate around surveillance of Muslim communities and protest permit restrictions remains within normal democratic parameters.
  • Not flagged by any major international rule-of-law monitoring body.
Regime / political instability
Low
Structural
  • No active no-confidence motion, coalition breakdown, or defection threats reported.
  • Government is 7 weeks old and operating normally; legislative priorities have been set without incident.
  • Minority status reflects how the government was formed, not evidence of imminent collapse.
Interstate tensions
Low
Structural
  • NATO and EU member in good standing; Jetten government is actively pro-EU and pro-NATO.
  • No active bilateral disputes of consequence.
  • Deepening cooperation with Belgium and Benelux partners on energy, defence, and semiconductor supply chains.
Belgium
PM De Wever "Arizona" coalition · In office since Jan 2025
Security Domain
Civil unrest
Low
Structural
  • Labour strikes and demonstrations are union-organised, scheduled in advance, and overwhelmingly non-violent.
  • De Lijn transport strikes running across Flanders this week (7–10 Apr) — expect public transport disruption, not disorder.
  • The 12 March Brussels demonstration (80,000 participants) had a brief, isolated confrontation near the US Embassy; quickly contained, not indicative of a broader pattern of clashes.
  • Pro-Palestinian demonstrations near diplomatic areas continue; peaceful and monitored by police.
Political violence & terrorism
Moderate
StructuralEmergingME Spillover
  • CUTA Level 3/4 "Serious" — NZ MFAT reconfirmed 9 April; attacks assessed as "possible and probable."
  • Crowded venues, transport hubs, and EU/NATO institutions named as specific target categories by CUTA.
  • HAYI-attributed attacks: Liège synagogue bombing (9 Mar), Antwerp Jewish quarter car burning (24 Mar) — zero casualties in both incidents.
  • Belgium deployed military to guard Jewish sites from 16 Mar; heightened police presence near Brussels diplomatic and transport hubs this week.
  • No indiscriminate attacks on general civilian or business targets in the past 1–2 weeks.
  • Jewish community remains the primary identified target set; general public infrastructure is not under named threat.
Violent crime
Moderate
Structural
  • Brussels has the second-highest murder rate in the EU, driven by drug trafficking through the Port of Antwerp.
  • 2025 saw a record 96 shootings in Brussels — a chronic structural issue, not an acute escalation.
  • Violence is concentrated in specific residential areas; not typically directed at business travellers or diplomatic visitors.
  • No acute escalation this week.
Political Domain
Rule of law
Low
StructuralEmerging
  • Functioning independent judiciary; no systemic concerns around arbitrary detention.
  • Controversial draft law approved 3 Apr 2026 would allow police with judicial warrant to enter homes for deportations — drawing civil society rights concerns.
  • Legislation subject to normal Council of State and parliamentary scrutiny; not flagged as a systemic failure by EU or Council of Europe monitoring bodies.
Regime / political instability
Low
Structural
  • No active no-confidence motion, coalition breakdown, or defection threats reported at the federal level.
  • De Wever's coalition has been functional since January 2025 and has weathered sustained labour unrest without political crisis.
  • Brussels-Capital regional governance deadlock (600+ days without a government) is a sub-national dysfunction and does not threaten federal government survival.
Interstate tensions
Low
Structural
  • NATO and EU member; hosts both headquarters in Brussels.
  • No active interstate disputes of consequence.
  • Minor diplomatic row with the US in Feb 2026 over ambassador comments on antisemitism was resolved quickly.
  • Belgium–Netherlands bilateral relations remain among the closest in Europe.
